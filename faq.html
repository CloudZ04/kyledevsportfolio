First and foremost, consultation is free. However, it is important to note that the consultation is not a guarantee I will accept your project. If you have applied for a consultation through my contact form, I will get back to you as soon as possible and we can arrange a time that is convenient for both of us.

In-person, call, or email?

I don't have an office, therefore I cannot offer in-person consultations. However, I can offer consultation via call or email:

  • Call (Zoom, Microsoft Teams, or Discord):

  • Email:
    • Perfect for those who prefer written communication or are shy like myself. Each accepted project will have a unique inbox for communication regarding the project - I suggest using the same method to keep all communication in one place if your Mailing Application allows.

Prices vary depending on the scope of the project. Once we work out the details of your project, you will be given a quote for the project. To help with this you should provide me with as much information as possible about your project, including your budget and timeline, as this will help me to give you a more accurate quote.

If you disagree with the quote and want to discuss changes, we can do so during the consultation phase; after consultation, the quote will remain the same unless additional work is required in which case, the quote will go up. Once you have agreed to the quote, you will be required to pay a deposit of 30% of the total project cost. This is to cover for early cancellation fees and is non-refundable. The remaining 70% will be due upon completion of the project.

What if I (the developer) cancels the project? As you know, sometimes life can get in the way and I may need to delay or cancel the project and take a step back. If this does happen we will discuss the situation about a possible delay. If you or I decide to cancel the project, you will be refunded the deposit paid in full.

There's nothing wrong with not liking the site I have designed, or changing your mind altogether about the project. However, please do be aware of the following:

  • Don't like the site currently?
  • I will work with you to make changes to the site until you are 100% satisfied. It would be helpful if you could provide me with a list of changes you want to make, and any other relevant information.

  • You changed your mind?
  • If you have changed your mind and no longer want to continue with the project, that's okay! However, you will be charged for the work that has been done so far. For example, if you have paid £1200 GBP for the website, and I have completed 30% of the project, then you will be charged £360 GBP. This is to cover my time and the resources used to build the site.

I have split this into 3 parts as it is very long. The 'Must have' section is required for any project. The other two are to simply help me understand more about your project and needs.
I will need the following information to build your site:

  • Must have:
  • Your brand and industry (including logo if applicable)
  • Your target audience (Who the site is for and what they need to do on it)
  • Your competitors (Helps identifying opportunities and avoiding copying what already exists)
  • Your content (Text, images, videos, etc.)
  • Your project goals and objectives (What you want to achieve with the site)
  • Your timeline for the project (Any fixed deadlines or launch dates)
  • Your estimated budget for the project (Helps scope features appropriately and helps with estimating pricing)

  • Nice to have:
  • Your design preferences (Examples of websites you like and dislike)
  • Your previous website(s) (If applicable - please provide links to the website)
  • Your brand font(s) (If applicable - otherwise I will choose a suitable font for your brand)
  • Your brand colour(s) (Websites often use 60% neutral/background colour, 30% for your structural/brand colour, 10% for call-to-action/accent colours. If no colours are given, I will choose suitable options for you to choose from.)
  • Functionality requirements (Forms, booking systems, payments, user accounts, administrative tools to manage the site, etc. If none of these requirements are given, they will not be implemented.)

  • Advanced / Protections:
  • SEO considerations (Keywords, meta descriptions, alt text, etc.)
  • Analytics and tracking (Google Analytics, etc.)
  • Legal requirements (Privacy policy, cookie banner, accessibility requirements (e.g. WCAG))
Please note that any legal requirements such as the privacy policy, may be dependent by country and industry, and should be reviewed by a legal professional before being implemented.

What if it is a WordPress website?

This will depend on the chosen method of building the WordPress site. If it is client-owned hosting, then admin access will just be handed back over to you as this is your host. If the site is developed locally or on a temporary environment, it will be transferred to your chosen hosting provider once complete, and full administrator access will be provided at handover.

Once the project is complete, responsibility for the website, including hosting, updates, security and maintenance, is fully transferred to you.

Basic documentation will be provided to help you manage the site. As WordPress is a widely used platform, there are also many high-quality resources available online, and it is recommended that you familiarise yourself with the system to get the most out of your website.
